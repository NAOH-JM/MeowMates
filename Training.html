8 Tips for training your cat

Use Treats as Motivation

Cats need a reason to learn! Stock up on their favorite treats to keep them engaged.

Stay Consistent

Use the same cues every time, and make sure everyone in the family does too.

Provide a Scratching Post

To save your furniture, offer a scratching post. Double-sided tape can also help deter scratching.

Choose a Quiet Spot

Cats are easily distracted, so train them in a calm, quiet space away from noise and people.

Keep Training Sessions Short

Cats have short attention spans, so limit training to 10-15 minutes...

This keeps your cat focused and prevents them from losing interest.

Train One Skill at a Time

Cats can get overwhelmed if you teach multiple skills at once.

Focus on one command, and only move on once they’ve mastered it.

Use Positive Reinforcement

Focus on rewarding good behavior instead of punishing bad behavior.

Praise, treats, or a gentle pet when your cat does something right will encourage them to repeat that behavior.

Choose the Right Time to Train

Cats are more focused and responsive right after they wake up from a nap or when they’re feeling playful.

Try training them when they're alert and a bit hungry, as they'll be more motivated by treats.
